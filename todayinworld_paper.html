Russia pardons group of prisoners Wagner

Thành Lê

Wagner announced the first group of 20 Russian prisoners to be pardoned after completing a contract to participate in "special military operations" with the corporation.

"They worked according to their contract, with honor and dignity," Wagner leader Yevgeny Prigozhin announced on January 5. This group of former prisoners was pardoned on the condition that they complete a six-month contract to fight in the military campaign in Ukraine.

The Wagner Group said the team consisted of 20 people, and during their service, they received less compensation than other Wagner contractors. Those injured in combat receive an additional stipend.

Prigozhin said these former prisoners "unintentionally have to sit behind bars or because of their nature, they cannot find a peaceful life and get into trouble".
The leader of the Wagner security group Yevgeny Prigozhin and former prisoners are pardoned in this photo released on January 5. Photo: RIA Novosti.
"They went forward, unafraid and looked the enemy straight in the eye," Prigozhin said. "These are natural-born warriors and they realize this. There is a conflict going on and they have to defend their homeland."

Leader Wagner stressed that Russian society gives former prisoners a chance and "should be grateful because they fight for the interests of the country". Once pardoned, former inmates can continue to work at Wagner as regular volunteers, many of whom said they plan to do so.

"We want to go back to finish what we started and continue to defend our homeland. Let's defeat the fascists like our forefathers did," a military contractor Wagner declared.
The situation of the war between Russia and Ukraine after more than 10 months of fighting. Graphics: WP.
The Wagner Group was founded in 2014 and has about 8,000 employees as of April 2022. Western intelligence considers Wagner contractors "Russian mercenaries", saying they have fought in Syria, the Central African Republic, Libya and Mali.

During a press conference in December 2022, US National Security Council spokesman John Kirby said that Wagner Group deployed about 50,000 military contractors to Ukraine.

Wagner contractors are considered to be better equipped and more experienced than some units of the Russian military. They are believed to mainly carry out small tasks, which is different from the large-scale operation of the Russian military.
