The US House of Representatives continues to fail to elect the president

Vu Anh(According to Reuters)

The Republican-controlled US House of Representatives has not yet elected a speaker, when candidate McCarthy did not receive enough support after six rounds of voting.
Candidate Kevin McCarthy after the vote in the US House of Representatives on January 3. Photo: Reuters.
Republican candidate Kevin McCarthy on January 4 continued to fail in the votes to elect the speaker of the House of Representatives, leaving the agency without choosing a new leader after six rounds of voting in the past two days.

McCarthy's allies are trying to negotiate behind the scenes to help him meet the 218 minimum votes to become House speaker, as 20 Republicans refuse to support McCarthy on the grounds of his lack of confidence. ideological trust and ignore calls for unity from former President Donald Trump.

McCarthy still maintains an optimistic mentality in the face of recent failures. "We will have 218 votes when we solve the problem and come together," he said.
Republican Leader of the House of Representatives Kevin McCarthy speaks in Washington, D.C. on November 15, 2022, after being nominated as speaker of the House. Photo: AFP.
"We're going to have to have a real conversation with a specific group of people, or drag everyone into the room and punch each other," Congressman Jeff Van Drew, a McCarthy supporter, told reporters.

Until a speaker is elected, the House of Representatives is largely paralyzed, unable to hold oaths for members, appoint committee chairmen or carry out other procedures. The last time the US House of Representatives failed to elect a speaker at the first vote was in 1923, when the body went through nine rounds of voting to choose a new leader.

McCarthy từng là lãnh đạo phe thiểu số tại Hạ viện và chạy đua trở thành chủ tịch kế nhiệm bà Nancy Pelosi, vị trí có quyền lực thứ hai trong danh sách kế hoạch vị trí tổng thống. Tuy nhiên, ông chống lại sự phản đối mạnh mẽ từ cánh hữu của đảng Cộng hòa.

The situation may force lawmakers to consider another candidate. A close associate of Mr. McCarthy, Steve Scalise, could be a potential candidate for the Speaker of the House.
Republicans won a majority in the House of Representatives with 222 seats, 10 more than Democrats, following the midterm elections last November.
