Checking and discovering the source of salary reform

Edan Nguyễn

Through the audit in Ho Chi Minh City, based on the data at 31/7/2022, it was determined that the excess source of salary reform in districts and districts is nearly VND 2,000 billion, not timely recovering the source of 10% savings of the estimating units.
Auditing the local budget in 2021 in Ho Chi Minh City, the State Audit of Region IV has proposed to handle finance of VND 3,294 billion (Illustration).
In addition, the IA also pointed out many shortcomings and limitations in budget revenues and basic construction investment expenditures of Ho Chi Minh City. Notably, in terms of compliance with the Law and the collection regime at tax payers, the results of inspection and comparison of 281 enterprises have: 239 enterprises with errors and untimely in complying with tax declaration and payment.

The IA identified and proposed to increase state budget revenue by VND 276.5 billion, reduce input VAT deducted by VND 23.9 billion, reduce the declared enterprise loss of VND 556.6 billion; 26 enterprises showed signs of improper tax declaration. In addition, there are 9 enterprises that do not provide sufficient documents for tax reconciliation in violation of Article 68 of the Law on IA, 2 enterprises that have petroleum trading activities but do not have business licenses as prescribed.

Through reviewing the declaration dossier of 30% reduction in corporate income tax (CIT) under Decree No. 92 of the Government, there are 352 enterprises declaring incorrectly subjects to be reduced according to regulations, the amount of CIT payable is an additional 7.7 billion VND.
Resolution 128 has instructed agencies and localities to create sources of wage reform
According to the KTNN, the regular spending of Ho Chi Minh City still has many shortcomings that need to be overcome. Specifically, in the field of budget, the District People's Committee is an estimating unit under the City People's Committee, leading to the failure to ensure timely funding for the implementation of urgent tasks, national defense, security, prevention, prevention and recovery of natural disasters, epidemics and unexpected tasks in the area (due to the absence of local budget surpluses).

In the implementation of the specific financial mechanism according to Resolution 54/2017/QH14 of the National Assembly, up to now, some contents have been implemented slowly compared to the plan, not achieving the set goals. According to the audit agency, in the condition that the city has implemented the organization of urban government, it is necessary to continue to propose to the National Assembly to promulgate a new resolution in accordance with the urban government mechanism, and at the same time have solutions and research to maximize mechanisms and policies such as: Decentralize management of a number of areas of investment management, budget finance; economic management; specific mechanisms and policies for the development of Thu Duc City...

It is worth noting that in terms of wage reform (CCTL), the 2021 budget still exists. Specifically, the city assigns estimates to estimating units and districts to balance recurrent expenditures (base salary difference of VND 280,000) and income increase according to Resolution 03/2018/NQ-HDND not in order of priority of funding sources as prescribed.

Through the audit, based on the data at 31/7/2022, it is determined that the excess supply of cctl sources of districts and districts (after excluding the source used to increase income according to Resolution No. 04 of the City People's Council in 2022 and the number of IAs proposed the previous year) is VND 1,938 billion; have not timely recovered 10% of savings of budget estimating units at all levels as prescribed in Circular No. 109 of the Ministry of Finance; the balance of cctl sources from state budgets of city departments and sectors must recover VND 184.4 billion, because the budget has not been fully adjusted to reduce cctl sources as prescribed.
According to budget revenue and expenditure data up to July 31, 2021, there are 10 districts and districts with budget revenue shortfalls, but the city budget has not allocated enough funds for COVID-19 prevention and control of districts due to some spending regimes not ensuring sufficient funding facilities, however, the People's Committees of districts and wards have not submitted to competent authorities appropriate financial handling plans as prescribed.
